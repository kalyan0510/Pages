Picking a timer device

First of all, what's with pulsar stars? These are dead stars that rotate very fast and emit a lot of light and other stuff along their axis of rotation and that axis when it sweeps our direction, gives us a visible pulse. That pulse is very consistent, and likely wont change in our life time. However, we cannot use them during day time. So this is not an option.

Now, we have water dripping and pendulums as viable options for our time measurement. What‚Äôs better?

Can I replicate a water dripping recurrency easily? Like, if I have a faucet that leaks at 1 drop per second, is it easy to make another leaky faucet that also drips 1 drop per second? Maybe it is, but as we keep copying to more faucets the error gets propagated. However, with pendulums, the frequency is dependent on the length of it. Two pendulums with the same length swing at the same frequency. It is easy to copy length. So let's go with Pendulum.

Well, I can make a string out of tree fibers and make an arbitrary frequency pendulum and call it a second. Who is keeping the time any way, post that apocalyptic event. I can define my own second ü§™
